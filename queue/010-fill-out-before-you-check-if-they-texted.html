Fill This Out Before You Check If They Texted Back

Here's the moment that ruins your own data every time: you get in the car, and before you can even ask yourself how that actually went, your thumb is already unlocking your phone to see if they texted. Whatever you felt for the ninety minutes before that is now getting overwritten by whatever that screen says.

If they texted "had a great time," suddenly the awkward pause at dinner didn't happen. If they went quiet, suddenly the whole night was a red flag parade. Neither read is honest. Both are you outsourcing your own gut check to someone else's reply time.

Your first read is the real read

The moment you step out of that restaurant or that car, your nervous system already has an opinion. It knows if you were performing a more agreeable version of yourself. It knows if that one joke landed wrong. It knows if you felt like you again by the end, or if you felt like you were interviewing for something.

That opinion has a shelf life of about an hour. After that, memory starts editing — usually in whatever direction the next text pushes it. So the rule is mechanical, not emotional: write it down before you check the phone, not after. Three lines is enough. A number out of ten is enough. You're not writing an essay, you're taking a reading before the instrument drifts.

One date tells you about a person. Three tell you about a pattern

Here's the part almost nobody tracks: the flag that shows up on date one and date three isn't a coincidence anymore — it's a preview. But you can only catch that if date one is actually written down somewhere other than your memory, which by date three has already started being generous.

Most people can tell you exactly how a situationship ended. Almost nobody can tell you what their gut said on date one, because nobody wrote it down while it was still true. That's the entire gap this closes.

The one-page version

You don't need a journal. You need a gut-check number, a flag checklist you can tick in ten seconds, and a single verdict: again, maybe, or never again. That's it. Do it in the car. Do it before the good-morning text arrives and negotiates with you.
